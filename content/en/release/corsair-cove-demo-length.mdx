Managing your workforce is split between two classes: Drifters and Pirates. Drifters perform general labor, such as hauling materials and constructing buildings, while Pirates are required for defense and sailing. Balancing their housing, food, and recreational needs is the core loop of the island management phase.

| Building Type | Construction Cost | Primary Function | Crew Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pirate Camp** | Free (Initial shipwreck) | Central resource hub and construction node | None |
| **Woodcutter** | 10 Planks | Gathers raw hardwood from nearby forests | 2 Drifters |
| **Sawmill** | 15 Hardwood | Processes raw hardwood into construction planks | 3 Drifters |
| **Galley** | 20 Planks | Converts raw food ingredients into cooked meals | 2 Drifters |
| **Tavern** | 30 Planks, 10 Booze | Distributes alcohol to raise crew satisfaction | 1 Pirate |

<Callout type="tip" title="Warehouse Efficiency">
Always build your warehouse centrally between your production buildings (like the sawmill) and your consumption buildings (like the pier). This minimizes the distance drifters must walk to store and retrieve heavy planks.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Naval Combat & Crew Recruitment">

Once you construct your first ship, the "Land Lover", the gameplay shifts from city-building to turn-based RPG exploration. Before you can set sail, you must recruit a minimum of 10 crew members. This requires converting idle drifters into active sailors at your tavern or camp.

The naval combat system utilizes a tactical dice-rolling mechanic. When you encounter an enemy vessel, your ship's stats determine how many action dice you roll during your turn. These dice are used to activate specific abilities, such as firing cannons, repairing hull damage, or maneuvering out of the enemy's line of fire.

| Combat Stat | Function | How to Improve | Importance Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hull Integrity** | Ship health; reaching zero results in defeat | Use repair skills in combat | ★★★★★ |
| **Dice Pool** | Number of actions available per turn | Upgrade ship tier or hire officers | ★★★★☆ |
| **Supplies** | Consumed as you travel across the sea map | Buy at ports or salvage shipwrecks | ★★★★☆ |
| **Crew Strength** | Determines boarding combat effectiveness | Recruit higher quality pirates | ★★★☆☆ |

During combat, you must carefully balance offensive maneuvers with defensive repairs. The enemy AI will actively target your weak spots, making it necessary to utilize your dice pools efficiently. Some choices during sea events will also affect your supplies and crew cohesion, adding a layer of survival management to your journeys.

<Callout type="warning" title="Supply Depletion">
Running out of supplies while sailing the world map will rapidly drain your crew's cohesion and health. Always ensure your cargo hold is stocked with food and water before leaving the safety of the cove.
</Callout>

</Section>
