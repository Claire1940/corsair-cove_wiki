Success in the early game depends on your ability to manage limited space and resources. Unlike traditional city builders, this game emphasizes a "cluttered" aesthetic where every building must be linked to the main grid. If a building loses its connection, production halts immediately, and your pirates will quickly grow restless.

<Callout type="tip" title="Connection Logic">

For instance, logs from the Wood Cutter must be processed at a Sawmill to create Planks, which are the primary currency for construction.

<Section icon={Users} title="Managing Pirate Cohesion and Morale">

In the **corsair cove demo**, your crew is not just a workforce; they are a volatile group of criminals. The **Cohesion** meter tracks the overall stability of your society. If this drops to zero, you face a mutiny. Maintaining high cohesion requires fulfilling basic needs: housing, food, and alcohol.

To manage your crew effectively, you must balance the needs of "Drifters" (unassigned labor) and "Pirates" (specialized crew). Events will frequently force you to make hard choices that impact your cohesion.

| Event Type | Typical Choice | Cohesion Impact | Resource Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stranded Spies** | Execute them | Moderate Loss | -2 Pirates |
| **Stranded Spies** | Welcome them | High Loss | None (Gain Workers) |
| **Scabies Outbreak** | Use Liquor to dull pain | Neutral | High Beer Drain |
| **New Refugees** | Accept Able-bodied | Small Gain | Food Upkeep |
| **New Refugees** | Rob them | High Loss | +100 Gold |

<Section icon={Anchor} title="Ship Construction and Naval Combat">

Once your base is stable, the **corsair cove demo** shifts focus to the high seas. Building your first ship, the Land Lover (a basic sloop), is a major milestone. This requires a Pier and a dedicated crew of Swabbies. The game features a unique captain system where different leaders provide distinct advantages in combat.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Construct the Pier">
    Place the Pier in a designated coastal slot and connect it to your main warehouse via a cliff path.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assemble the Crew">
    Convert Drifters into Swabbies. This requires an upfront gold cost and a steady supply of food.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Assign a Captain">
    Choose a captain like **Teach** for aggressive playstyles or **Honorata** for exploration and treasure hunting.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Embark on Missions">
    Select targets on the world map, such as Skull Rock or Barbos Bay, to plunder resources like Black Powder and Gold.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Combat is resolved through a dice-based card system. Your captain's traits determine your dice pool, and the outcome of your rolls dictates the effectiveness of your actions, such as "Aim and Fire" or "Wasteful Barrage."

| Combat Action | Dice Requirement | Effect | Supply Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aim and Fire** | Single Die | Deals 4-9 Damage | Low |
| **Heavy Fire** | Double Dice | Double Damage to Health | Moderate |
| **Take Cover** | Defensive Roll | Prevents 3-8 Damage | Low |
| **Wasteful Barrage** | High Roll | Massive AOE Damage | Very High |

<Callout type="info" title="Captain Teach">
Captain Teach is the premier choice for the demo's combat missions. His "Easy as Murder" trait grants an extra dice roll in the next turn if you deal damage, allowing for devastating combos.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Defense and Fortification">

As your infamy grows, the Spanish Crown, led by the hunter Amara, will launch retaliatory strikes against your cove. Defensive structures are required to protect your infrastructure. The **Gun Tower** is your primary defense, but it requires a complex supply chain involving Stone Blocks and Black Powder.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Stone Production Chain">
    1. **Quarry**: Extract raw stone from cliffs.
    2. **Stone Cutter**: Refine stone into Blocks.
    3. **Transport**: Use porters to move blocks to the construction site.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Black Powder Logistics">
    1. **Guano Gatherer**: Collect saltpeter from bird nesting sites.
    2. **Harbor Storage**: Store powder away from residential tents to prevent accidents.
    3. **Gun Tower**: Requires a constant supply of powder to fire at enemy ships.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

During an attack, the Gun Tower operates automatically as long as it is supplied. You must ensure your porters can reach the tower safely during a bombardment. If a tower runs out of Black Powder, it becomes a useless pile of rocks, leaving your Tavern and Galley vulnerable to destruction.

<Callout type="tip" title="Defensive Placement">
Place your Gun Towers on jutting outcrops. This maximizes their firing arc and keeps the "splash damage" from enemy cannons away from your vital food and wood production buildings.
</Callout>

</Section>
