### Managing Bottlenecks
To ensure your settlement doesn't collapse, follow these optimization rules:
1. **Shorten Routes**: Move processing buildings closer to the raw resource nodes.
2. **Free Labor**: Pause low-priority construction to return Drifters to the labor pool.
3. **Local Supply**: Ensure every Pirate Camp has its own food and drink distribution to prevent long-distance "fetch" trips.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Naval Combat and Fleet Management">

Combat in Corsair Cove is a strategic, turn-based affair. Unlike the real-time chaos often found in other pirate games, this system relies on dice pools, captain abilities, and careful supply management.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Ship Selection">
    Compare ship stats (Health, Crew, Supplies) against the event's recommended ratings. The **Steel Edge** is superior for combat, while the **Landlubber** is a basic starter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assign a Captain">
    Match captain traits to the objective. For example, **Teach** grants extra dice after dealing damage, making him ideal for aggressive encounters.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Dice and Actions">
    Spend Supplies to play Battle cards like **Heavy Fire** or **Into the Breach**. Use Support cards to recover Supplies when they run low.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Countering">
    Watch the enemy's planned action. You can fully negate their damage if you play a card targeting the same area with a higher dice roll.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Combat Tip">
Always return to port after a major victory. Chaining events without refilling Crew and Supplies is the fastest way to lose a high-tier ship.
</Callout>
