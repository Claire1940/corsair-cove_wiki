<Section icon={Target} title="Managing Complex Production Chains & Layouts">

Logistics are the lifeblood of your pirate haven. In the **corsair cove new game**, resources do not automatically teleport to where they are needed. Instead, a dedicated class of workers known as "fetchers" must physically carry items from extraction sites to processing buildings, and finally to distribution hubs. 

Because flat land is scarce, you must build pathways along cliffsides. If a building is not connected to the central logistical grid via ladders or bridges, its workers will become idle, and your economy will stall.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Crew Satisfaction, Cohesion, and Progression">

A pirate crew is only as loyal as their stomachs are full. The game features a prominent "Cohesion" meter that represents the collective morale of your society. If cohesion drops to zero, your crew will mutiny, resulting in violent duals, sabotaged buildings, and potentially a premature end to your campaign.

To maintain high cohesion, you must satisfy the evolving demands of your population. While basic drifters are content with simple tents and hot stew, advanced specialists and elite corsairs require luxury goods like tobacco, fine attire, and pocket-sized firearms.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Basic Shelters">
    Construct tents near the central pirate camp. This provides shelter for newly arrived drifters and prevents early-game drop-offs in cohesion.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Secure Food & Ale">
    Build a galley to process raw ingredients into hearty stew, and set up a moonshine still to supply your local tavern with high-proof alcohol.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Construct the Pier">
    Build a shoreline pier to begin assembling your first scouting vessels. This allows you to send fleets out to raid nearby islands for resources you cannot produce locally.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Train Specialists">
    Upgrade your housing to attract specialized crew members like navigators and bombardiers, who are essential for commanding larger warships.
  </Step>
</Steps>
