<Section icon={Shield} title="Community Feedback & Gameplay Systems">

As players dive into the demo, active discussions regarding core gameplay mechanics have emerged. The community serves as a vital sounding board for balancing the game's intricate systems, which range from complex production chains to tactical combat encounters.

While the city-building mechanics have received widespread praise, some systems have sparked healthy debate among strategy enthusiasts.

- **Combat System**: The game utilizes a card and dice system to resolve naval encounters rather than real-time ship battles. Some players appreciate the tactical depth, while others miss active sailing mechanics.
- **Labor Force Management**: Managing "drifters"—the basic labor force—is a common progression bottleneck. Players on Discord frequently share tips on how to efficiently attract and retain these workers.
- **Building Variety**: From standard alehouses to controversial structures like the cockfighting ring, building choices directly impact your crew's behavior and your settlement's overall reputation.

| Issue | Community Sentiment | Potential Solution |
|---|---|---|
| No Active Sailing | Highly debated among RTS fans | Focus on mastering the card and dice combat systems |
| Drifter Shortage | Frustrating during early game | Optimize housing availability and keep morale high |
| Combat RNG | Some players report bad luck | Invest in dice mitigation upgrades and better cards |

<Callout type="warning" title="Progression Warning">
Failing to maintain a steady influx of drifters can completely lock your progress. Prioritize basic housing and food production before expanding into advanced military structures.
</Callout>

</Section>
