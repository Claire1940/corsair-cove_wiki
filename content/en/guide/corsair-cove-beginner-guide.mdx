<Section icon={Map} title="Vertical Building and Spatial Optimization">

Building a pirate settlement requires a smart approach to space management. When you first arrive, flat ground is extremely limited. The small beach areas can only hold a few basic structures, forcing you to think creatively about your layouts. To build a successful settlement, you must utilize the verticality of your island. Slopes, cliffs, and rocky formations are not obstacles; they are valuable building zones that help you maximize your territory.

**Video Highlights:**
- Flat ground is highly limited, making vertical construction essential from the start.
- Building on cliffs and slopes reduces walking distances and travel times for your pirates.
- Housing structures can be placed in isolated areas like Skull Rock without affecting efficiency.

<YouTubeEmbed videoId="mSpVcDtCvFA" title="Corsair Cove Beginner's Guide EP1 – Cohesion, Progression, Unlocks & How to Win" />

By constructing your settlement vertically, you significantly reduce the walking paths of your pirates. Shorter paths mean faster resource transportation and higher overall productivity. Furthermore, housing does not need to be placed near production buildings. Your crew members only require a roof over their heads, meaning you can place residential quarters on isolated cliffs or directly on top of Skull Rock, saving valuable flat ground for logistics and production.

| Zone | Space Availability | Best Building Type | Path Efficiency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Beach** | Very Low | Docks & Warehouses | High for logistics |
| **Cliffs** | Moderate | Production facilities | High for short paths |
| **Skull Rock** | High | Pirate housing | High for isolation |

<Callout type="tip" title="Building Tip">
Always prioritize placing production and storage facilities close together on flat ground, while sending residential buildings to the cliffs. This minimizes travel time for working pirates and keeps your economy running smoothly.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Managing Crew Cohesion and Unity">

Cohesion is the core metric that determines the stability of your pirate crew. It measures the strength of your crew's unity and their willingness to work together toward a common goal. If cohesion drops to zero, conflicts escalate, cooperation breaks down, and your pirates will abandon the settlement, resulting in an immediate game over. Keeping this metric stable is your highest priority during the early game.

Conversely, maintaining high cohesion provides you with a valuable strategic resource. You can spend cohesion to take powerful actions, resolve crises, or secure unique advantages. However, keeping cohesion high often requires making difficult ethical choices. As a leader, you may need to sacrifice individual crew members to preserve the unity of the group. Understanding when to make these sacrifices is key to keeping your crew functioning as a single, united force.

| Cohesion Level | Status | Effects | Management Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High (80-100)** | Excellent | Boosts strategic actions | Maintain via victories |
| **Medium (30-79)** | Stable | Standard operation | Monitor deed completion |
| **Low (1-29)** | Critical | Increased conflict | Sacrifice crew members |
| **Zero (0)** | Game Over | Settlement dissolves | Avoid at all costs |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Do not hesitate to sacrifice a troublesome pirate if your crew's cohesion drops to critical levels. Losing a single worker is always better than facing a total collapse of your settlement.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="The Four Paths to Victory">

In Corsair Cove, your ultimate objective is defined by your progression menu, which is accessed through the journal tab inside the compass menu. There are four distinct paths you can follow to complete the game and secure your legacy. Each path focuses on a different aspect of pirate life, allowing you to tailor your gameplay experience to your preferred style.

  <Card title="Notoriety Path">

  <Card title="Empire Path">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Seafaring Path">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Wealth Path">

You do not have to commit to a single path at the start of the game. You can progress along all four quest lines simultaneously, completing objectives and earning rewards from each. It is only at the very final stage of the game that you must make a definitive choice on how to complete your run. This flexibility allows you to experiment with different mechanics before deciding on your ultimate victory condition.

| Path | Focus | Key Unlock | Playstyle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Notoriety** | Combat & Infamy | Flagships | Aggressive raiding |
| **Empire** | Fortifications | Bastions | Defensive building |
| **Seafaring** | Exploration | Navigation charts | Active sailing |
| **Wealth** | Trade & Gold | Treasury vaults | Economic focus |

<Section icon={Target} title="Deeds and Compass Progression">

Your progression is driven by the completion of deeds, which are small quests awarded for almost every activity you perform in the game. Whether you are optimizing your logistics network, building new ships, or raiding enemy fleets, you will constantly earn points for completing these tasks. These points are then invested directly into your compass to unlock new content.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Check the Journal">
    Open your progression journal by clicking the compass icon on your screen to view your active main quests.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Complete Minor Deeds">
    Focus on minor tasks like building basic shelters and setting up logistics to earn initial compass points.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Unlock New Blueprints">
    Spend your earned compass points in the progression tree to unlock advanced buildings and ship designs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Engage in World Events">
    Look for events with colored banners on the sea map to earn bonus points and accelerate your progression.
  </Step>
</Steps>

In addition to standard deeds, you can earn substantial compass points by participating in world events. When exploring the world map, look for events marked with colored banners. These events offer unique challenges and high-value rewards that can significantly accelerate your progression, allowing you to unlock advanced ship blueprints and powerful building structures much faster.

| Category | Deed Example | Compass Points | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Logistics** | Construct roads | 15 Points | Faster resource transport |
| **Seafaring** | Explore unknown tiles | 25 Points | New ship blueprints |
| **Shipbuilding** | Launch first sloop | 30 Points | Fleet expansion |

<Callout type="success" title="Progression Tip">
Prioritize logistics deeds in the early game. Improving your resource transport early on makes completing later, resource-heavy deeds much easier.
</Callout>
