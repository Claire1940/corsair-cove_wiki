To secure a stable foundation, you must master the initial construction phase. When you decide to establish a new pirate camp, the very first action you should take is to pause the simulation. This allows you to plan and place your infrastructure without wasting precious passive resources or letting crew satisfaction tick downward.

The core of any successful camp layout revolves around four critical buildings: the Moonshine Still, the Campfire, the Galley, and the Tavern. These structures must be placed in a tight, interconnected cluster to minimize transit times for your workers.

| Building | Primary Input | Output | Satisfaction Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Pirate Camp | None | Basic Housing | Low |
| Moonshine Still | Sugarcane | Moonshine | High |
| Campfire | Wood | Warmth & Safety | High |
| Galley | Raw Food | Rations | Critical |
| Tavern | Rum / Alcohol | Happiness | Critical |

By placing the Galley and the Tavern directly above and below the Moonshine Still and Campfire, you create an incredibly fast supply loop. The workers do not have to walk across the entire camp to deliver raw ingredients, ensuring that your crew's basic needs are met almost instantly. To complete this setup, construct at least six basic tents nearby and ensure they are fully connected via roads to the central hub. This layout practically guarantees 100% satisfaction, protecting your outpost from any risk of low morale or productivity drops.

<Callout type="tip" title="The Pause Trick">
Always press the spacebar to pause the game immediately when establishing a new camp. This gives you unlimited time to configure your supply loops and guarantees that your crew never experiences a single second of undersupply.
</Callout>

This approach is highly inefficient, as it leads to redundant buildings, wasted space, and fragmented labor. By analyzing the local environment using the resource overlay, you can dedicate entire islands to a single major industry. One camp can serve as your agricultural breadbasket, another as your military arsenal, and a third as your commercial distillery.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Shipbuilding & Crew Upkeep Chains">

Your fleet is the ultimate projection of your pirate power. However, constructing advanced hull types—such as Destroyers, Hunters, Prowlers, and Reavers—is only half the battle. Each ship class unlocks access to specialized, elite crew members who offer massive stat bonuses. These elite crews are not free; they require unique, high-tier upkeep resources that must be manufactured back at your camps.

For example, hiring Picaroons requires a steady supply of Rum, which in turn requires you to establish a sugarcane farm, a cooperage for barrels, and a distillery. Hiring Scallywags requires Cassava Bread, necessitating a fully functional agricultural pipeline.

  <Step num="1" title="Unlock the Hull Class">
    Construct a Destroyer or Prowler shipyard to unlock advanced crew slots.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify Upkeep Needs">
    Check the crew hover-tips to see if they require Rum, Cassava Bread, or Medicine.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Establish the Raw Farms">
    Build Sugarcane or Cassava farms on specialized islands with high soil fertility.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Construct Processing Facilities">
    Build Coopers for barrels, Distilleries for Rum, or Bakeries for Cassava Bread.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Route to Taverns">

  </Step>
</Steps>

Elite crew members fundamentally alter how your ships perform in battle. While default Swabbies provide no statistical advantages, specialized crew members can turn a standard vessel into an unstoppable warship.

| Crew Type | Upkeep Resource | Primary Stat Bonus | Ideal Ship Class |

Before recruiting these high-performing units, always ensure your economy can support their ongoing upkeep. A sudden deficit in Cassava Bread or Rum can cause your elite crews to lose their bonuses, severely weakening your fleet during critical engagements.

<Callout type="warning" title="Upkeep Bottlenecks">
