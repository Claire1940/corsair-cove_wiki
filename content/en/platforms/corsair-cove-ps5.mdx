<Section icon={Users} title="Crew Morale and Production Lines">

A pirate is nothing without their crew. In **corsair cove ps5**, keeping your crew happy is just as important as building a fast ship. Unhappy crews lead to mutinies, reduced production efficiency, and failure during critical naval engagements. You must run a tight ship by setting up automated production lines that cater to the needs of your settlement and your fleet.

The production system is hierarchical. Raw materials gathered from the environment are sent to processing buildings, which then feed into manufacturing hubs. Managing this flow is the key to scaling your pirate empire from a single raft to a fleet of galleons.

| Crew Role | Key Responsibility | Morale Impact | Skill Progression |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Quartermaster** | Resource distribution | High | Logistics and Trade |
| **Boatswain** | Ship maintenance/repairs | Medium | Engineering |
| **Gunner** | Cannon accuracy and reload | Medium | Combat and Ballistics |
| **Cook** | Food quality and variety | Very High | Survival and Health |
| **Carpenter** | Building speed and durability | Low | Construction |

<Callout type="warning" title="Mutiny Warning">
If morale drops below 20%, your crew may seize a ship and leave your settlement, taking valuable resources with them.
</Callout>

Managing these roles involves assigning the right people to the right tasks based on their individual traits. Some crew members may be expert builders but poor sailors, requiring you to balance your land-based workforce with your naval personnel.
