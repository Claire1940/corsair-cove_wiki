When you first land on Skull Rock, your immediate priority is salvage. The shipwreck contains the vital planks needed to kickstart your economy. Using the flexible road-building tool, you can draw pathways up steep cliffs and automate the placement of wooden ladders. This verticality is a core mechanic of the game, allowing you to utilize every level of your island's geography.

Your population consists initially of drifters. Drifters are general laborers who can be assigned to specialized roles. These include builders, working hands, fetchers, and crew members. Managing this workforce dynamically is key to keeping your economy balanced as you expand from a tiny camp into a massive pirate city.

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategic Road Placement">
Always draw your road connections directly to resource nodes early on. This ensures your fetchers can transport salvaged planks and logs to the warehouse without unnecessary travel delays.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Production Chains and Resource Management">

To sustain a growing pirate population, you must establish efficient production chains. The economy is split into basic survival goods and advanced industrial materials. Basic goods keep your pirates fed and happy, while advanced materials are required for shipbuilding and coastal defense.

Your first major production chain is wood processing. You will need a Woodcutter to harvest logs, which are then processed by a Sawmill into planks. Planks are the primary building material for almost every structure in your haven. Following wood, food production must be established. This requires a Campfire to cook raw ingredients into stew, and a Galley to distribute the food to your crew.

| Output | Input | Key Building | Worker Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Planks** | Logs | Sawmill | Working Hands |
| **Stew** | Raw Food | Campfire | Fetchers |
| **Beer** | Yeast, Water | Brewery | Working Hands |
| **Canvas** | Fiber, Wooden Nails | Weaver | Working Hands |
| **Sails** | Canvas | Sailmaker | Working Hands |

As your city grows, your pirates will demand entertainment. This introduces the alcohol production chain. You must construct a Brewery to produce beer and a Tavern to serve it. Keeping your pirates well-fed and sufficiently drunk is the only way to maintain high satisfaction levels. 

Advanced production chains involve crafting sails and ropes for your fleet. This requires a Leaf Cutter to gather fiber, a Wood Workshop to craft wooden nails, a Weaver to turn fiber and nails into canvas, and finally a Sailmaker to produce sails. 

| Advanced Resource | Required Inputs | Processing Chain | Primary Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sails** | Canvas | Leaf Cutter to Weaver to Sailmaker | Upgrading and building ships |
| **Ropes** | Fiber | Rope Maker | Naval progression and rigging |
| **Stone Blocks** | Raw Stone | Quarry to Stone Cutter | Constructing defensive towers |

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch Your Food Supply">
Do not expand your workforce too quickly. A sudden influx of drifters will deplete your stew reserves rapidly, causing satisfaction to plummet and triggering an early uprising.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Cohesion, Housing, and Defense Strategies">

Your pirate haven is constantly under threat from within and without. Cohesion represents the collective morale of your crew. If cohesion drops to zero, your crew will mutiny, resulting in an immediate game over. Random narrative events will test your leadership. For example, capturing enemy soldiers forces you to decide between executing them for a minor cohesion loss or recruiting them, which gains you workers but severely damages overall trust.

Housing is directly tied to satisfaction. Pirates demand proper shelter, which you provide by building tents. Tents can be clustered together along the cliffs to save space. 

Additionally, you must prepare for external threats. The Spanish hunter Amara will periodically locate your island and launch devastating naval bombardments. To survive these attacks, you must transition from a pure economy to a militarized state by building Gun Towers.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Shipbuilding, Captains, and Naval Combat Walkthrough">

To truly master the seas, you must construct a fleet. Shipbuilding requires a Shallow Pier, which can only be placed in predefined coastal locations. Once your pier is built, you can establish a shipyard to construct your first vessel, the humble Land Lover.

As you progress through the tech trees, you will unlock advanced ship blueprints. These ships vary in speed, crew capacity, and combat capabilities.

| Ship Blueprint | Crew Capacity | Boarding Damage | Speed Rating | Special Attributes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Land Lover** | 15 | Low | Slow | Basic starter vessel |
| **Moonlighter** | 20 | Medium | Moderate | Enhanced cargo capacity |
| **Tide Runner** | 25 | Medium | Fast | High evasion and speed |
| **Destroyer** | 40 | High | Moderate | Heavy hull plating |

Every ship requires a captain and a crew. You can choose from several unique captains, such as Teach (Blackbeard), who specializes in exploration and gun damage. Assigning a full crew of drifters to your ship increases your chances of success during world map voyages.

Naval combat in the game is a tactical, turn-based card and dice-rolling affair. When you engage an enemy ship or raid a port, you must manage your crew, supplies, and hull health.

| Combat Action Card | Dice Requirement | Core Effect | Tactical Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Safe Distance** | High Roll | Prevents incoming damage | Use when enemy prepares a heavy barrage |
| **Into the Breach** | Balanced Roll | Deals high crew damage | Perfect for preparing to board the enemy |
| **Heavy Fire** | High Roll | Deals massive hull damage | Best used against armored merchant escorts |
| **Windfall** | Any Roll | Generates extra loot and supplies | Play when the enemy is in a defensive stance |

<Section icon={Target} title="The Four Winds: Progression Trees and Deeds">

Progression is governed by "The Four Winds" system. This massive progression tree is split into four distinct paths: Seafaring, Empire, Notoriety, and Wealth. You earn progression points by completing specific achievements known as Deeds.

Deeds range from simple tasks like gathering 100 total pirates to complex challenges like maintaining three efficient transport routes simultaneously.

| Progression Path | Key Unlocks | Playstyle Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Seafaring** | Tide Runner ship, Sailmaker efficiency | Exploration, speed, and naval dominance |
| **Empire** | Ziplines, stone quarries, larger camps | Rapid expansion, infrastructure, and logistics |
| **Notoriety** | Gun towers, advanced cannons, combat cards | Defensive power, raiding, and combat prowess |
| **Wealth** | Trading posts, gambling dens, coin generation | Passive income, market trading, and luxury goods |

By carefully choosing which deeds to pursue, you can customize your pirate haven to match your preferred playstyle. For example, focusing on the Empire path allows you to unlock advanced transportation systems like ziplines and cliffside elevators, which drastically reduce the time your fetchers spend moving resources up and down the island's steep cliffs.
