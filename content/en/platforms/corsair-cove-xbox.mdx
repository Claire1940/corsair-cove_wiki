<Section icon={Shield} title="Production Chains & Crew Management">

A pirate haven cannot survive on plunder alone. You must establish efficient production chains to supply your crew with food, rum, and ammunition. If your crew becomes unhappy due to poor working conditions or lack of supplies, productivity drops, and the risk of mutiny increases.

Managing resources involves setting up automated pathways. For example, raw sugar cane harvested from farms must be transported to a distillery to produce rum. This rum is then delivered to taverns to maintain crew morale.

| Raw Material | Processing Facility | Finished Product | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Raw Timber** | Sawmill | Wood Planks | Construction & Ship Repair |
| **Sugar Cane** | Distillery | Fine Rum | Crew Morale & Trading |
| **Iron Ore** | Smelter | Iron Ingots | Weapons & Ship Cannons |
| **Hemp Fiber** | Weaver | Sailcloth | Ship Construction |

Keeping your crew happy requires balancing their workloads with adequate recreation. Ensure that housing is kept away from noisy industrial areas, and keep the local taverns well-stocked with rum.

<Callout type="warning" title="Mutiny Warning">
If crew happiness drops below 30%, workers may stop producing resources and attempt to steal gold from your treasury. Always monitor the morale meter.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Naval Construction & Exploration Steps">

Once your settlement is secure and your production lines are stable, you can begin constructing ships to explore the Seven Seas. Ships allow you to trade with distant ports, hunt for treasure, and engage in naval combat against rival factions.

Building and launching your first vessel requires following a specific sequence of steps to ensure your ship is sea-ready and properly crewed.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Construct the Dockyard">
    Select a clear coastal area with deep water access to build your basic dockyard. This serves as the foundation for all maritime construction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Gather Ship Materials">
    Collect the required wood planks, sailcloth, and iron ingots. Deliver these materials to the dockyard storage chest to begin the construction process.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Select a Blueprint">
    Choose the vessel type you wish to build. Early players should start with a small, agile ship before attempting to build larger warships.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Assign a Crew & Provisions">
    Assign idle settlement workers to the ship's crew. Stock the cargo hold with food, fresh water, and cannonballs before setting sail.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Different ship types serve different purposes. While small ships are ideal for fast exploration and trading, larger ships are necessary for heavy combat and carrying large amounts of plunder back to your cove.

| Ship Type | Crew Size | Cargo Capacity | Combat Rating |

<Callout type="success" title="Exploration Tip">
