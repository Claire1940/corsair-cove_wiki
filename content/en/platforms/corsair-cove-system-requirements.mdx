<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Vertical City Building and Hardware Performance">

Corsair Cove is not a traditional, flat city builder. The game allows players to build up cliff faces, connect islands via massive bridges, and transport resources vertically using elevators and ziplines. This verticality introduces unique processing challenges that affect both your CPU and GPU.

As your population grows, hundreds of Drifters, Builders, and Fetchers move goods through your logistics networks. The CPU must calculate real-time pathfinding routes across multiple vertical tiers. If your logistics lines turn red due to long, inefficient routes, your CPU workload increases significantly.

To keep your system running cool during massive expansions, consider adjusting specific graphics settings that impact system performance.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Performance Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shadow Quality** | Medium | Reduces GPU overhead in dense cliffside districts |
| **Crowd Density** | Medium | Lowers CPU utilization during peak pirate activity |
| **Water Details** | High | Preserves ocean aesthetics with minimal GPU cost |
| **Path Visualization** | Off | Disabled by default to reduce CPU pathfinding strain |

<Callout type="tip" title="Optimize Your Logistics">
Keeping your production routes short and green does more than just improve your economy; it also reduces the CPU cycles spent on pathfinding calculations. Use localized Pirate Camps and distribution hubs to keep your hardware running smoothly.
</Callout>

</Section>
