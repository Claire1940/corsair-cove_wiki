<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: How to Create and Assign Fetchers">

Creating an efficient workforce requires a systematic approach to camp expansion. While the game handles much of the micro-assignment, your layout dictates how these fetchers behave. Follow these steps to ensure your logistics are flawless from the moment you drop your first anchor in 2026.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Pause and Plan">
    Before placing any structures, press the **Spacebar** to pause the game. This allows you to place the Pirate Camp, Moonshine Still, and Campfire simultaneously. By planning the layout while paused, you ensure fetchers don't start inefficient routes before the full production chain is ready.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish the Core Four">
    Place the **Tavern** and **Galley** in a central location. Directly above or below these, place the **Moonshine Still** and **Campfire**. This "vertical" or "horizontal" alignment creates the shortest possible path for fetchers to deliver essential goods.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Expand the Labor Pool">
    Build a minimum of six tents connected to your main camp. The game will automatically draw from this population to create fetchers. If you notice buildings are "Waiting for Resources," it usually means your labor pool is too small or the distance is too great.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Assign Production Specialization">
    Instead of making every camp produce everything, assign specific camps to specific roles. For example, designate one island for **Cassava Bread** and another for **Gunpowder**. This simplifies the fetcher routes, as they only have to move one type of resource to the local export dock.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Worker Shortage">
If your satisfaction drops below 50%, fetchers may stop working or move slower. Always prioritize the "Core Four" buildings to keep the workforce motivated.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Advanced Haulage Strategies">

As your empire grows, you will need to manage multiple islands. The game's AI is generally smart about assigning fetchers, but you can "force" better behavior through clever road placement and building clustering. In the 2026 version of Corsair Cove, pathfinding favors straight lines over diagonal ones.

**Key Strategies for Large Colonies:**
- **Hub and Spoke**: Create a central storage hub where all fetchers deliver raw goods, with secondary fetchers moving them to processing.
- **The "Pause" Method**: Whenever you add a new link to a production chain, pause the game. This resets the AI's "nearest task" logic and often results in more efficient fetcher assignments.
- **Ship Recommendations**: Don't always follow the game's "Recommended Ship" for events. If your current fleet has higher stats (like armor or boarding strength), use it even if the game suggests a different class. This saves resources that fetchers would otherwise spend on repairing or building new ships.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Strategy">
If a camp is struggling, check the "Travel Time" stat on your production buildings. If it's over 20 seconds, you need to move your tents or storage closer to the workplace.
</Callout>
