<Section icon={Swords} title="Naval Combat and Dice Mechanics">

Engaging the Crown requires a mastery of the turn-based combat system. When a **corsair cove manowar** enters an encounter, the game shifts to a strategic interface where cards represent your tactical options. Each turn, the enemy reveals their intent, giving you a chance to counter or push for your own objectives.

The flow of combat is governed by dice pools. Your ship's ratings provide the base number of dice, while your Captain's abilities and active Battle cards can add "bonus dice" to the roll. Success is not just about rolling high; it is about managing your **Supplies** so you don't run out of actions during the final phase of a battle.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Analyze Event Objectives">
    Check if the goal is to sink the enemy, close the distance, or extract resources. Match your ship's ratings to the icon's recommendations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Battle Cards">
    Use **Battle cards** like "Heavy Fire" (costs 2 Supplies) to deal damage to enemy Health or "Into the Breach" to target the enemy Crew.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute Counter Actions">
    If the enemy is using an "All-out Barrage," use defensive cards like "Fall Back" to negate damage and gain bonus dice for the next turn.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage Supply Recovery">
    When Supplies drop below 5, switch to **Support cards**. These often provide no damage but allow your crew to restock essential munitions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Combat Success">
Winning a battle yields high-value loot like **Silver**, **Wine**, and **Whale Blubber**, which are essential for satisfying elite pirate tiers in your settlement.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Users} title="Captain Traits and Crew Management">

No **corsair cove manowar** is complete without a legendary captain. Captains are not just flavor; they provide passive perks and unique cards that can turn the tide of a losing battle. For example, **Teach (Blackbeard)** specializes in aggressive maneuvers, granting extra dice whenever he deals damage to enemy Health.

Managing the crew on these massive vessels also requires a stable settlement. Larger ships require **Specialist Sailors** like Bombardiers and Navigators. These specialists have higher needs than your basic Drifters, demanding goods like **Spyglasses**, **Medicine**, and **Fancy Attire**.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Production Chains for Heavy Warships">

Building and maintaining a **corsair cove manowar** requires the most complex production chains in the game. Unlike early-game buildings that only need Wood and Stone, warships require a steady supply of refined metals and military-grade explosives. If your logistics network has a "Red" distance bottleneck, your shipyard will stall, leaving your cove vulnerable.

The following table outlines the primary resources needed to keep your fleet operational and your settlement protected by defensive towers.

| Resource | Source Building | Input Materials | Final Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Iron Plating** | Iron Foundry | Iron Ore + Charcoal | Hull Reinforcement |
| **Cannons** | Cannon Smithy | Iron + Copper | Ship & Tower Armament |
| **Black Powder** | Powder Mill | Sulfur + Charcoal | Ammunition for Guns |
| **Hardwood Planks** | Sawmill | Ancient Timber | Tier 2+ Ship Frames |
| **Slops & Boots** | Tailor / Cobbler | Fiber + Leather | Basic Crew Upkeep |

<Callout type="warning" title="Logistics Warning">
Always place your **Cannon Smithy** and **Powder Mill** near a **Distribution Hub**. These goods are heavy, and long transport routes will significantly slow down your ship repair speeds.
</Callout>
