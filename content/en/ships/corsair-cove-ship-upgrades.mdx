<Section icon={Map} title="Unlocking Ship Upgrades via the Seafaring Principle">

In the vertical pirate city builder Corsair Cove, expanding your territory and securing resources is only half the battle. To establish true dominance over the Seven Seas and resist the oppressive Crown, you must transition from a simple harbor settlement into an empire with a formidable fleet. This progression is governed by the Compass system, which splits your progression paths into four distinct philosophies: Notoriety, Empire, Seafaring, and Wealth. 

To access the most powerful **corsair cove ship upgrades**, you must focus your efforts on the Seafaring principle. Earning Principle Points through Deeds and Side Quests allows you to climb the Seafaring progression tree, unlocking specialized ship blueprints, construction options, and direct performance upgrades at the Pier.

| Principle | Focus | Fleet Impact | Key Unlock |
|---|---|---|---|
| Notoriety | Pirate reputation and fear | Unlocks aggressive questlines | Terror tactics and combat bonuses |
| Empire | Settlement growth and society | Increases population caps | Advanced housing and utility buildings |
| Seafaring | Ships, voyages, and exploration | Unlocks advanced hulls and upgrades | Steel Edge blueprint and rating boosts |
| Wealth | Production, trade, and economy | Funds ship construction | Resource production multipliers |

Investing heavily in Seafaring is the most direct route to transforming your starter vessels into high-tier warships. While a balanced approach across all four Compass paths provides a versatile settlement, specializing in Seafaring early ensures that your fleet can comfortably tackle higher-tier world events and defend your home port from retaliatory Crown attacks.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Do not neglect Deeds in your quest log. Many mid-game Deeds award concentrated Seafaring Principle Points, which bypasses the standard progression grind and lets you unlock Tier 2 ship hulls much faster.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Ship Stats and Upgrade Comparisons">

Every ship in your fleet is defined by its core survival metrics and five tactical event ratings. Before sending a crew out on a World Map expedition, you must compare your ship’s upgraded ratings against the recommended metrics displayed on the event icon. Standard vessels can be upgraded at the Pier, shifting them from fragile utility boats into heavily armored destroyers.

The starter ship, the Landlubber, serves as a baseline vessel. It is cheap to maintain but lacks the durability and rating capacity to survive beyond early-game encounters. Upgrading to a Tier 2 vessel like the Steel Edge requires an investment of advanced materials but drastically increases your fleet's survival rate in turn-based combat.

| Ship | Tier | Class | Health | Max Crew | Supplies | Construction Cost | Core Use |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Ship Upgrade Process">

Upgrading your fleet requires a functional harbor infrastructure and a steady flow of refined materials. Follow this step-by-step progression route to successfully commission and upgrade your vessels at the Pier.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Earn and Invest Principle Points">
    Complete Side Quests and targeted Deeds to accumulate Principle Points. Open the Compass menu and invest these points into the Seafaring branch until you unlock the Steel Edge blueprint and associated ship rating upgrades.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish Refined Material Chains">
    Construct a Sawmill to process raw timber into Hardwood Planks. Ensure your Treasury is stocked with at least 50 Coin, which is acquired through trade, local taxation, or successful World Map raids.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Construct and Access the Pier">
    Place your Pier along a flat, connected shoreline. Ensure the Pier is linked directly to your settlement's main logistics spine via a short, green-rated route to minimize construction delays.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Commission the Upgrade">
    Select the Pier, open the ship construction menu, and select the Steel Edge or your desired rating upgrade. Assign a team of Builders to complete the construction. Keep enough Drifters free to avoid stalling other settlement tasks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Success">
Once construction is complete, your new vessel will dock at the Pier, ready to accept a captain, crew, and voyage supplies.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Equipment Production Chains and Logistics">

Maintaining an upgraded fleet requires more than just lumber and coin. Advanced warships and specialist sailors demand specialized military and luxury goods to operate at peak efficiency. If these goods are not consistently supplied to your Pier and Pirate Camps, crew satisfaction will plummet, and Cohesion—your global survival meter—will begin to decay.

To prevent supply bottlenecks, you must construct dedicated production chains that feed directly into your harbor logistics. The distance between your production buildings, distribution hubs, and the Pier is represented by colored route lines: green routes are highly efficient, yellow routes are suboptimal, and red routes indicate severe transport delays that can stall your shipbuilding efforts.

| Good | Raw Materials | Production Building | Target Fleet Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hardwood Planks | Raw Timber | Sawmill | Basic hull construction and repairs |
| Cannons | Iron Ore, Coal | Foundry | Weapon upgrades and heavy combat ratings |
| Black Powder | Sulfur, Charcoal | Powder Mill | Munitions supply for battle card actions |
| Spyglasses | Glass, Copper | Workshop | Specialist Sailor upkeep and scouting |
| Medicine | Herbs, Clean Water | Apothecary | Crew healing and high-risk expedition survival |

Managing these resources effectively ensures that your upgraded ships never sit idle at the dock due to a lack of ammunition or supplies. Keep your military production hubs clustered near the coast, and use distribution hubs to route finished goods directly to the Pier.

<Callout type="info" title="Logistics Tip">
If a red route warning appears on your Pier, do not build duplicate factories. Instead, construct a Bridge or place a Zipline to bridge vertical cliff gaps, shortening the physical path your Fetchers must travel.
</Callout>

For example, Captain Teach is an exceptional choice for combat-focused vessels like the Steel Edge. His passive perk, Hatred Is Power, grants a massive +20% boost to both Ship Health and Gun Damage, directly scaling with the base stat upgrades you apply at the Pier.
