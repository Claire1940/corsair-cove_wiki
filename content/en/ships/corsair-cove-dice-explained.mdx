<Section icon={Swords} title="Corsair Cove Dice Explained: Mastering Combat RNG">

The most frequent question for new captains involves the **corsair cove dice explained** mechanics within the encounter system. Combat is not a real-time skirmish but a calculated card-battler where every action is influenced by a roll of the dice. When you engage in a multi-stage battle at sea, your success depends on your ability to manage your supply of resources while playing the right cards at the right time.

Every card in your hand has a designated damage or defense range. When a card is played, the game triggers a dice roll to determine where the actual result falls within that range. A "High" roll hits the upper limit of the card's potential, while a "Low" roll results in minimum impact. This creates a high-stakes environment where even the best-laid plans require a bit of luck.

| Card Type | Primary Effect | Supply Cost | RNG Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attack** | Deals damage to ship or crew | Medium to High | High (Wide damage range) |
| **Defense** | Mitigates incoming enemy damage | Low to Medium | Moderate (Fixed block + roll) |
| **Sacrifice** | Trade ship health for supplies | Zero | Low (Fixed supply gain) |
| **Tactical** | Buffs next roll or debuffs enemy | Medium | N/A (Utility focused) |

<Section icon={Target} title="Pirate Crew Management and Cohesion">

Your workforce in Corsair Cove is not a group of disciplined citizens; they are unpredictable cutthroats. Managing your crew is a constant balancing act between greed and survival. If your pirates become unhappy or if you make too many decisions that favor gold over their well-being, your **Cohesion** meter will drop. If this meter hits zero, your crew will mutiny, resulting in an immediate game over.

Pirates have varying needs based on their specialization. While basic workers only require food and grog, high-tier specialists who buff your ships demand much more expensive luxuries.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Economic Stability and Resource Chains">

The economy in Corsair Cove is built on production chains that must be perfectly balanced. Because production buildings have very limited internal storage, any bottleneck in your logistics network can cause the entire chain to grind to a halt. You are responsible for directing the flow of goods, deciding which warehouses receive specific finished products and where raw materials are sourced.

The game uses a color-coded efficiency system to help you visualize these flows. By monitoring your paths and buildings, you can quickly identify where your pirates are struggling to move goods effectively.

**Efficiency Indicators:**
- **Green**: Optimal flow; resources are moving without delay.
- **Yellow**: Minor bottlenecks; consider upgrading paths or adding winches.
- **Red**: Critical failure; production has stopped due to full storage or lack of inputs.
