Infrastructure is connected via bridges and specialized connectors that allow your pirates to traverse different levels of the island.

The **Corsair Cove AI** handles the pathfinding for your pirates, but poorly designed bridge networks can lead to long travel times. If a fetcher has to cross three different cliffs to deliver moonshine to the tavern, your efficiency will plummet.

| Infrastructure | Benefit | Limitation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

<Section icon={Users} title="Managing Pirate Units and Production Chains">

Your population consists of two primary roles: **Hands** and **Fetchers**. Hands are the muscle—they work inside buildings like the Sawmill or the Moonshine Still. Fetchers are the brains of the operation, moving resources between production points and storage. Understanding the **Corsair Cove AI** logic for these units is the key to a thriving economy.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Wood Production">
    Place a Woodcutter to generate hardwood. Follow this with a Sawmill to turn that hardwood into Planks, which are required for almost every early-game building.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Secure the Food Supply">
    Build a Campfire to process raw ingredients and a Galley to serve the food. Without a steady food source, your pirates' happiness will drop, reducing their work efficiency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Initiate Moonshine Production">
    Construct a Moonshine Still. Moonshine is the primary currency for pirate happiness and is essential for recruiting crew members at the Tavern.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Activate Overdrive">
    Use the 'All Hands on Deck' toggle for critical buildings. This provides a 50% production boost but doubles the number of Hands required, which can strain your workforce.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Resource | Source | Use Case | AI Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hardwood** | Woodcutter | Raw material for Planks | Medium |
| **Planks** | Sawmill | Construction / Shipbuilding | High |
| **Food** | Campfire / Galley | Sustenance / Happiness | Critical |
| **Moonshine** | Moonshine Still | Recruitment / Tavern | High |
| **Stone** | Quarry | Advanced Construction | Low (Early) |

<Callout type="success" title="Efficiency Tip">
Right-click on a fetcher's route to manually prioritize or disable specific resource transfers. This allows you to force-feed resources to a building that is lagging behind.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Ship} title="Naval Expeditions and Captain Selection">

Once your island is stable, you must take to the seas. Shipbuilding is done at the Shallow Pier. This opens up the "Adventure" phase of the game, where you engage in events like smuggling runs, merchant raids, and naval combat. The success of these missions depends on your ship's stats and the Captain you choose to lead them.

The expedition system uses a card-based mechanic influenced by dice rolls. You must manage three vital resources during any voyage:
1. **Crew**: Your actual population. Losing them means fewer workers back on the island.
2. **Health**: The structural integrity of your ship. At zero, the ship is lost.
3. **Supplies**: Consumed during events. You can trade Health or Crew to gain more Supplies in an emergency.

| Captain | Primary Bonus | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blackbeard** | Increased Notoriety / Combat | Raiding Merchant Vessels |
| **Kanja** | Speed / Wealth Generation | Smuggling & Trade Runs |
| **Edward Teach** | Empire Building Synergies | Long-term Island Expansion |
| **Generic Captain** | Balanced Stats | Early Exploration |

<Callout type="info" title="Expedition Logic">
During events like a Smuggling Run, the **Corsair Cove AI** calculates advantage based on your cards. Always look for cards that "Prevent Loss of Advantage" to save your precious supplies for the final stage of the event.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Progression, Deeds, and Cohesion">

Progress in Corsair Cove is not just about building more houses; it is about completing "Deeds." Deeds provide unlock points that you can spend in the tech tree to access better ships, advanced buildings like the Gambling Den, and infrastructure upgrades like Zip Lines.

**Cohesion** is a unique resource that represents the social fabric of your pirate community.
- **High Cohesion**: Provides leeway during setbacks and disasters.
- **Zero Cohesion**: Your pirate group dissolves, resulting in an immediate Game Over.
- **Trade-offs**: You will often face choices—rob a group of pirates for gold (increases cohesion) or recruit them (decreases cohesion but increases population).
