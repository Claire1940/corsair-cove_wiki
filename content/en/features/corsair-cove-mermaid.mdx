<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner's Guide: Your First Hour in the Cove">

Starting your pirate haven can be overwhelming. You begin with a small group of survivors and limited resources. The goal is to establish a self-sustaining economy while preparing for the eventual arrival of the Crown. Following a structured progression path ensures you don't run out of essential supplies like Hard Tack or Ale in the early game.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Download the Free Demo">
    Before the July 31 release, visit the Steam store to install the free demo. This allows you to complete the initial tutorial and familiarize yourself with the vertical construction tools and the UI.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish the Harbor Core">
    Place your first Pirate Camp near the starting pier. Connect essential service buildings immediately. A building that is not connected to the settlement spine will not function, indicated by a red warning icon.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Balance Drifters vs. Workers">
    Drifters are your construction force. If you assign every pirate to a production job, you will have no one left to build new structures. Always keep a reserve of 3-5 Drifters for expansion.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Secure Food and Drink">
    Build a Fishery and an Alehouse early. Pirate satisfaction is heavily tied to these two resources. Without them, Cohesion will drop, eventually leading to a game-over scenario.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Logistics Warning">
Check your logistics lines frequently. Green lines indicate efficient short-distance transport. Red lines mean your Fetchers are walking too far, which will eventually bottleneck your entire production chain.
</Callout>

<Callout type="success" title="Efficiency Bonus">
Unlock the "Wealth" principle in the Compass menu to gain access to the Entertainers' Guildhall. This building boosts the productivity of all nearby production facilities, allowing you to produce more with fewer workers.
</Callout>

### Identifying Bottlenecks
Use the Resources panel to monitor production versus consumption. If the trend line is red, you are consuming more than you produce. This is often caused by:
1. Lack of raw materials at the source.
2. Fetchers being overwhelmed by long travel distances.
3. The destination building (like a Tavern) being full.

Captains like **Teach** or **Honorata** provide unique perks. For example, Teach's "Hatred Is Power" perk increases Gun Damage by 20%, making him ideal for aggressive raids. Always match your Captain's traits to the objective of the voyage.
