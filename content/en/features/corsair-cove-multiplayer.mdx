<Section icon={Swords} title="Simulating Co-Op: Interacting with AI Factions">

While direct **corsair cove multiplayer** is absent, the game's World Map features dynamic AI factions that bring the pirate world to life. Instead of cooperating with real players, you will coordinate with smugglers, rescue trapped prisoners, and assist local rebels resisting Crown rule. These interactions occur via World Map events, which require careful fleet preparation and captain selection.

Managing these relationships is key to securing resources that your home island cannot produce. For example, grain, coin, wine, and silver are often obtained by raiding Crown outposts or completing smuggling runs. Treating the AI factions as your cooperative partners or competitive rivals shapes your progression through the four Compass principles: Notoriety, Empire, Seafaring, and Wealth.

| Faction | Behavior | Interaction Type | Rewards |

| **Rebels** | Oppressed locals | Narrative choices, assistance | Cohesion boosts, specialized crew |

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Fleet Coordination Guide">

To make up for the lack of co-op partners, you must learn to manage multiple ships and crews simultaneously. Coordinating your fleet requires matching the right ship metrics and captain skills to specific World Map events. Sending an unprepared fleet into hostile waters will result in lost ships, dead crew members, and a massive drop in settlement Cohesion.

Follow this step-by-step process to prepare your fleet for high-risk voyages:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Analyze the Event Requirements">
    Inspect the World Map icon to determine the event type, recommended ship ratings, and potential hazards. Do not rely solely on the overall event level; compare individual metrics like hull strength and speed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select and Upgrade Your Ship">
    Choose a vessel that fits the task. For basic transport, the starter Landlubber is sufficient. For heavy combat, construct the Steel Edge at your Pier using hardwood planks and iron.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Assign a Specialized Captain">
    Match your captain's traits to the objective. For example, assign Captain Teach for combat missions to benefit from his damage boosts, or choose an explorer captain for ancient temple expeditions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Stockpile Supplies and Ammunition">
    Ensure your ship is fully stocked with food, water, and repair kits before departure. Additionally, keep your island's coastal fortifications supplied with cannonballs and black powder to defend the harbor while your fleet is away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Combat Readiness">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Crew Management and Settlement Cohesion">

In the single-player campaign, your crew acts as your cooperative workforce. You must balance the needs of various worker tiers to keep your settlement stable. Unassigned Drifters are required for construction, while assigned Workers and Specialists run your production chains. If their needs for food, drink, clothing, and entertainment are ignored, they will abandon your haven.

Cohesion is your global survival meter. If Cohesion drops to zero, your pirate society collapses, and the game ends. Controlling population growth and maintaining a steady supply of basic goods like hard tack and ale are essential for keeping Cohesion high.

| Crew Tier | Housing Requirement | Primary Consumption | Key Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Drifters** | Basic Pirate Camp | Hard tack, water | Construction, basic labor |
| **Workers** | Standard Cottages | Stew, basic ale | Resource extraction, farming |
| **Specialists** | Advanced Quarters | Medicine, fine clothing | Advanced manufacturing, sailing |
| **Elite Sailors** | Luxury Estates | Rum, tobacco, jewelry | High-tier naval combat, raiding |
